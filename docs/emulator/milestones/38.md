# Milestone 38 — Timed HD64180 `RET NC` Opcode Coverage for PacManV8 T020

Objective:
- Close the next real-ROM timed CPU compatibility gap exposed after M37
  unblocked the scene-1 intermission panel setup path. With `LD E,n`,
  `LD H,n`, and `LD L,n` now timed, the PacManV8 blocked task
  `/home/djglxxii/src/PacManV8/docs/tasks/blocked/T020-intermission-cutscenes.md`
  now renders the first intermission scene and advances past it. The
  canonical headless runtime then aborts while advancing from scene 1
  to scene 2 with:

  ```text
  Unsupported timed Z180 opcode 0xD0 at PC 0x2F75
  ```

- `PC=0x2F75` is inside `intermission_advance_review_index`. The failing
  instruction is standard Z80 `RET NC`:
  - If carry is clear: pop PC from stack, 11 T-states.
  - If carry is set: `PC += 1`, 5 T-states.
  - Flags untouched in either case.

  T020 genuinely needs it as the deterministic review-index guard so
  scene selection walks `1 → 2 → 3` without running past the last
  review scene. Rewriting the ROM around it would be an out-of-scope
  emulator workaround.

- The blocker's look-ahead for related active-path conditional returns
  confirms `RET C` (`0xD8`) is already used in other accepted runtime
  modules:
  - `src/ghost_house.asm`: `RET C` at lines 122, 173, 181, 189.
  - `src/movement.asm`: `RET C` at line 360.
  - `src/ghost_ai.asm`: `RET C` at line 392.

- `RET Z` (`0xC8`) and `RET NZ` (`0xC0`) are **already** executing in
  the intermission path prior to this failure per the blocker, so they
  are already timed and must not be touched.

- This milestone exists to close the `RET NC` gap — and, to avoid
  another rebuild-per-opcode loop on the same carry-conditional RET
  family, the sister form `RET C` (`0xD8`) — in one documented pass,
  matching the family-closure precedent established by milestones 34
  and 37.

Allowed scope:
- Timed opcode coverage inside the existing extracted HD64180 runtime
  for the missing carry-conditional `RET cc` forms directly implicated
  by the T020 blocker and its documented active-path look-ahead:
  - `RET NC` (`0xD0`) — the confirmed failing opcode at `PC 0x2F75`.
  - `RET C` (`0xD8`) — sister form in the same carry-conditional
    family, closed to prevent a follow-up rebuild cycle on known
    active modules (`ghost_house.asm`, `movement.asm`, `ghost_ai.asm`).
- Implementation shape must follow the existing pattern already used
  for the accepted `RET Z` / `RET NZ` handlers in
  `third_party/z180/z180_core.cpp` (test the flag, pop PC on true,
  advance PC on false; no flag updates).
- Adapter T-state timing entries for each new opcode added in this
  milestone:
  - Taken branch: **11 T-states**.
  - Not-taken branch: **5 T-states**.
  Register them in the existing `RET cc` timing groups in
  `src/core/cpu/z180_adapter.cpp` alongside the already-covered `RET Z`
  (`0xC8`) and `RET NZ` (`0xC0`).
- Tests and docs needed to lock the newly covered timed opcode surface.
- Narrow runtime/integration coverage proving the canonical PacManV8
  T020 minimal repro can run past `PC=0x2F75` without a timed-opcode
  abort, and that scene-2 and scene-3 frame captures and the
  intermission completion frame become reachable.

Explicit non-goals:
- **No general CPU opcode sweep.** No new arithmetic families,
  index-register (`DD`/`FD`) prefixes, `CB`-prefix work, or block
  opcodes.
- **No other conditional-RET forms.** `RET PO` (`0xE0`), `RET PE`
  (`0xE8`), `RET P` (`0xF0`), and `RET M` (`0xF8`) are not on any
  confirmed active path in the T020 blocker or its look-ahead and are
  explicitly out of scope. They will be addressed in a separate
  milestone if and when a real-ROM path exercises them.
- **No rewrite of existing `RET cc` handlers.** `RET Z` (`0xC8`) and
  `RET NZ` (`0xC0`) are already timed and must not be touched.
- **No interrupt-controller, scheduler, audio, VDP, or frontend
  changes.** This milestone is exclusively about adding the two
  missing carry-conditional RET opcodes and their T-state timing
  entries.
- **No PacManV8 ROM edits.** The T020 evidence refresh happens in the
  PacManV8 repo after this milestone lands.
- **No M35 observability-flag changes.** `--inspect`, `--inspect-frame`,
  `--peek-mem`, `--peek-logical`, `--dump-cpu`, `--dump-vdp-regs`,
  `--dump-vram-a`, `--dump-vram-b`, and `--run-until-pc` must behave
  identically before and after this milestone.
- **No HALT-wake changes.** M36 owns that path; its tests must stay
  green unchanged.
- **No changes to `LD r,n` coverage from M37.** Those handlers and
  their 7-T-state adapter entries must remain untouched.

Required tests:
- Focused timed CPU coverage in `tests/test_cpu.cpp` for each new
  opcode, pinning:
  - `RET NC` (`0xD0`) — **taken branch** (carry clear): `PC` is
    loaded from the top of the stack, `SP` advances by 2, no flags
    change, adapter reports 11 T-states.
  - `RET NC` (`0xD0`) — **not-taken branch** (carry set): `PC`
    advances by 1, `SP` unchanged, no flags change, adapter reports
    5 T-states.
  - `RET C` (`0xD8`) — **taken branch** (carry set): `PC` loaded
    from stack, `SP` advances by 2, no flags change, adapter reports
    11 T-states.
  - `RET C` (`0xD8`) — **not-taken branch** (carry clear): `PC`
    advances by 1, `SP` unchanged, no flags change, adapter reports
    5 T-states.
- At least one test mirroring the PacManV8
  `intermission_advance_review_index` pattern at `0x2F70..0x2F7A`:
  `LD A,(addr)` → `CP n` → `RET NC` → `INC A` → `LD (addr),A` →
  `RET`, exercising both the early-return and fall-through paths of
  `RET NC` and verifying the review-index advance semantics.
- Adapter-level T-state coverage confirming each new opcode is
  classified in the correct taken/not-taken timing case and that the
  overall adapter dispatch remains deterministic.
- Non-perturbation guard: for a chosen ROM, `--frames`, and hashing
  selection that does not exercise the new opcodes, frame and audio
  digests before and after this milestone must be byte-identical.
  Reuse an existing M33 / M35 / M37-verified fixture already present
  under `tests/`.
- Runtime/integration coverage that rebuilds the PacManV8 ROM and
  proves the canonical T020 minimal repro completes without a
  timed-opcode abort at `PC=0x2F75`, and that scene-2 (`--frames 1770`),
  scene-3 (`--frames 2520`), and intermission completion
  (`--frames 2640`) all produce stable, deterministic frame digests
  across repeat runs.

Exit criteria:
- The emulator no longer aborts on the documented PacManV8 T020 path
  with `Unsupported timed Z180 opcode 0xD0 at PC 0x2F75` (or at any
  `RET C` site in `ghost_house.asm`, `movement.asm`, or
  `ghost_ai.asm` reached by this ROM).
- The added timed opcode coverage for `RET NC` and `RET C` is narrow,
  documented, and regression-covered in both taken and not-taken
  branches.
- `ctest --test-dir cmake-build-debug --output-on-failure` passes with
  the new opcode tests included, and no pre-existing CPU test is
  relaxed or removed.
- Deterministic digests for ROMs that do not depend on the new opcodes
  are unchanged.
- PacManV8 T020 becomes actionable again from the emulator side; the
  actual cutscene evidence refresh (scene-2, scene-3, and completion
  frame captures) and acceptance still happen in the PacManV8 repo.

Forbidden extras:
- Turning this milestone into a broad `RET cc` sweep that includes
  parity or sign conditional returns.
- Bundling unrelated PacManV8 compatibility fixes under the same task.
- Loosening existing timed CPU or integration tests instead of adding
  the missing opcode coverage alongside them.
- Altering PacManV8 sources to route around the blocker.
